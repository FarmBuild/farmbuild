FarmBuild

A unique distribution model for agricultural science

FarmBuild is delivered by Agriculture Victoria, a division of the Victorian Government's Department of Economic Development, Jobs, Transport and Resources (DEDJTR).

What does FarmBuild do?

FarmBuild delivers agricultural science such as algorithms, calculators, and key agricultural datasets as online functions. An open source repository of web services and application programming interfaces (API) makes FarmBuild accessible to be used by anyone at any time. Web service APIs and associated data sources, and open source JavaScript API sample code and pages, are available for users to build their own digital tools and support their clients.

Current online functions available through FarmBuild include mapping, farm nutrient balance models and soil information. New functions and data delivery will be developed for FarmBuild over the coming years of the project. For more information please visit the Online functions page.

Who is FarmBuild for?

Commercial business and industry bodies will be the next users of FarmBuild. It is envisaged that most agriculture businesses will need to collaborate with software developers to be able to utilise FarmBuild effectively. The aim of the FarmBuild project is to work with industry bodies and software developers to facilitate the use and application of FarmBuild, and grow and develop additional components of FarmBuild based on the needs of our users.

Ultimately, the end users of the FarmBuild agriculture services are the farmers, land managers and advisors making decisions on farm to improve their agricultural businesses. Why is FarmBuild different?

This unique distribution model allows the Victorian Government to deliver agricultural research science more effectively, without the demands of creating and maintaining the digital tools that support on-farm decisions. Users such as agricultural service providers, who have greater on-ground knowledge of what science and technologies land managers require to improve on-farm practices, will be better equipped through FarmBuild to develop their own digital tools.

Why do we need FarmBuild?

The Victorian Government provides essential agricultural scientific research information and data to a wide audience, including farmers, land managers, agricultural businesses, the education sector, other research institutions and Government agencies. Victorian Government agricultural research has developed, enhanced and maintained a wide range of models, measurement tools and datasets to improve:

  • the use of farming inputs (e.g. water and nutrients);
  • soil health;
  • product yield and quality; and
  • offsite impacts.

FarmBuild provides a way to translate this science into practical, everyday decision support on-farm by leveraging digital technology and services.
