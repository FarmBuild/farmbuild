Delivering spatial information for improved management of individual farms, entire landscapes and sub-regions is critical for productivity improvement, land and water management and to support farm extension activities.

Driver for the Research

As part of the Department of Economic Development, Jobs, Transport and Resources (DEDJTR) on-line initiatives the FarmWeb 2.0 project will design, deploy and evaluate a web 2.0 spatial mapping and data management program to enable farmers to improve productivity while reducing their environmental impact. Agricultural industries are coming under increased external scrutiny from customers and regulators to demonstrate environmental compliance. Over the last decade DEDJTR has taken the lead in investigating the link between on-farm actions, productivity and environmental outcomes.

Research Focus

The initial focus of this work will be applications in the Dairy industry. DEDJTR will work collaboratively with Murray Goulburn Cooperative (MGC) Ltd to develop, trial and deploy a FarmWeb 2.0 on-line mapping and nutrient management tool with their farmers. The developed systems will aim to provide a two way flow of data between government, industry and farmers/landholders.

Project Objectives.

The objectives of the Gardiner project “FarmWeb 2.0: A web-based nutrient topic package for the Victorian Dairy Industry are: 1. To design, test and deploy, in conjunction with Murray Goulburn, a Web-based Nutrient Management Topic Package (as a case study for the wider dairy industry) to examine the potential to demonstrate on-farm best nutrient management practices. 2. To evaluate and communicate the usefulness and usability of a Web-based topic package as spatial information tools for better production and Natural Resource Management outcomes on Victorian dairy farms.

Farm Build Nutrient Calculator

This is the nutrient calculator module of farm build javaScript library.

A nutrient balance calculator for Australian dairy farms: In partnership with Murray Goulburn, DEDJTR has developed the science based ‘Dairy Farm Nutrient Balance Model’. This javaScript library can be used to model nutrient flow in and out of your dairy farm. Since Australian dairy farms generally import larger quantities of nutrients than they export, knowing the nutrient status of your farm gives you the potential to save money.

What is a nutrient balance calculator?

The nutrient balance model determines how efficiently a nutrient is being used on-farm. The amount of a nutrient leaving in farm products (milk or animals) is subtracted from nutrient inputs to calculate the nutrient balance (kg of nutrient per ha of land).

The Whole Farm Nutrient Balance Calculator works with yearly totals. The following is the list of functions provided in this modules:

  • Numbers of Animals purchased for the year (Heavy Cattle, Average Cattle, Yearlings, Weaned young stock and Bobby Calves).
  • Forage Purchased (Hay and Silage) (Amount Purchased (Tonne/yr) by type).
  • Concentrate Purchased (Amount Purchased (Tonne/yr) by type).
  • Fertiliser Purchased (Amount Purchased (Tonne/yr) by type).
  • Number of Sold and Culled Cows (Heavy Cattle, Average Cattle, Yearlings, Weaned young stock and Bobby Calves).
  • Milk Sold, Total Milk for Year (Litres) Total Fat/Protein (kg) Fat (kg/%) and Protein (kg/%).
  • Legume – Whole Farm average legume percentage (%) the percentage of pasture legumes growing of the farm.
